NOISE POLLUTION

Noise pollution, also known as environmental noise or sound pollution, isNoise pollution is generally defined as regular exposure to elevated sound levels that may lead to adverse effects in humans or other living organisms. According to the World Health Organization, sound levels less than 70 dB are not damaging to living organisms, regardless of how long or consistent the exposure is. Exposure for more than 8 hours to constant noise beyond 85 dB may be hazardous. If you work for 8 hours daily in close proximity to a busy road or highway, you are very likely exposed to traffic noise pollution around 85dB.

Causes Of Noise Pollution

  1. Industrialization
  2. Poor Urban Planning
  3. Transportation
  4. Construction Activities
